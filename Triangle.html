Equilateral triangle

In geometry, an equilateral triangle is a triangle in which all three sides are equal.

Isosceles triangle>

In geometry, an isosceles triangle is a triangle
that has two sides of equal length.
The other dimensions of the triangle
such as its height, area, and perimeter,
can be calculated by simple formulas from the lengths
of the legs and base.
Every isosceles triangle has an axis of symmetry,
along the perpendicular bisector of its base.
The two angles opposite the legs are equal and
are always acute, so the classification of the triangle
as acute, right, or obtuse depends only on the angle between its two legs.

Scalene triangle

A scalene triangle is a triangle that has three unequal sides,
The most important property of scalene triangles is that
they have three sides of different lengths.
however, they have some other important properties, too.
Like other triangles, all the angles inside a scalene
triangle add up to 180 degrees.
